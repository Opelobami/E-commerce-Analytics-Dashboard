# Project Overview
This project analyzes a global software retailer's e-commerce sales data, covering subscriptions and add-ons across analytics, design, collaboration, and AI product categories. The primary objective is to understand who the loyal (repeat) customers are, which channels and campaigns drive their return, and how pricing and promotions influence long-term revenue growth. The final deliverable is a three-page interactive Power BI dashboard designed for business stakeholders; clear, visual, and decision-ready.

---

## 🎯 Why This Project Matters
Most e-commerce dashboards tell you what happened.
This one tells you what to do next.
In subscription software, retention is revenue. This dashboard was built to answer the questions that actually move the needle:

- Which channels build loyalty and not just traffic?
- Are discounts growing the business or training customers to wait for sales?
- Where is revenue quietly leaking through refunds and wrong-fit plans?

Because knowing your numbers is one thing. Knowing what they mean is the job.

---

## #Business Question
1. How do total sales change by month?
2. Which channels bring in the most sales?
3. Which channels bring the most repeat (loyal) customers?
4. What percent of monthly sales comes from loyal customers?5
5. Which products or plans sell the most?
6. Which products are most popular with loyal customers?
7. How long do customers wait before their second purchase?
8. Which discount codes are used most and do they increase repeat purchases?
9. What is the average selling price (ASP) by country?
10. Where do refunds happen most; by product or channel?
11. Do annual plans bring higher revenue per customer than monthly plans?
12. Which channels and segments drive the most loyal customers?

## 💡 Key Insights
- Website drives everything - $4.12M in revenue and 3,851 loyal customers. It's not just the top channel, it's the retention engine.
- Annual plans are 10x more valuable than monthly ($1,197 vs $118 avg revenue per transaction).
- Loyal customers mostly buy without discounts; 3,983 of them used no promo code at all.
- France, Netherlands & Germany have the highest average order values. Shows that europe is the premium market.
- Customers return every ~42 days signifying a predictable loyalty cycle that's currently going untracked.
- Enterprise is only 2.83% of customers which is the highest-value segment that is virtually untouched.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaways
- Retention is already happening organically, but the business isn't capitalizing on it yet.
- The real revenue lever is plan type, not volume. Fewer annual customers beat more monthly ones every time.
- Discounts attract customers but don't create loyalty. Value does.
- Europe is the high-ticket market. It deserves a targeted strategy, not a generic one.
- The 42-day repurchase window is a predictable re-engagement opportunity being left on the table.

---

✅ Strategic Recommendations
- Invest more in the Website channel, optimize UX - user experience, post-purchase flows, and retention emails.
- Push annual plans at every touchpoint; trials, upgrades, and renewal reminders.
- Replace blanket discounts with value-led retention; onboarding, customer success, loyalty perks.
- Launch localized upsell campaigns in France, Netherlands, and Germany.
- Trigger re-engagement at day 35 post-purchase to capture the 42-day loyalty cycle.
- Build an enterprise sales motion - dedicated outreach, custom pricing, account management.
